# Standards and Regulatory Framework for Defining the Scope of Physical Disability in the UK

## Aprroved Document M
**Approved Document M** is a set of guidelines in the UK that provides practical guidance on building regulations to ensure access and inclusion for all. It is divided into **two volumes**:

**Volume 1**: Covers dwellings and provides guidance on accessibility features.

**Volume 2**: Covers buildings other than dwellings and includes requirements for accessibility.

The document was first published in **March 2015** and has been updated with amendments since then. 

## Equality Act 2010
The Equality Act 2010 is a key piece of UK legislation that protects individuals from discrimination based on characteristics such as disability, age, gender, and race. For disability, the Act requires service providers—including healthcare facilities—to make **“reasonable adjustments”** to ensure that disabled people are not placed at a substantial disadvantage compared to non-disabled individuals.

## BS 8300
BS 8300 is a **British Standard** providing best practice guidance for the design of accessible and inclusive built environments. It is divided into two parts: **BS 8300-1:2018**, which focuses on external environments such as pedestrian routes and access to buildings, and **BS 8300-2:2018**, which addresses the internal design and use of buildings, including entrances, circulation, and facilities. In this project, BS 8300 is used to define and operationalise accessibility features into measurable variables, supporting the development of facility and route-based accessibility models for healthcare services.

Note: Full access is available for BS 8300-1:2018, while only a preview (review version) is available for BS 8300-2:2018.

## Inclusive Mobility
Inclusive Mobility is official guidance published by the UK government that outlines best practices for designing streets and pedestrian environments to be accessible for disabled people, particularly those with mobility impairments. It covers key aspects of the outdoor built environment, including footways, gradients, crossings, street furniture, and surface conditions.
